About the Artist

Tom Fruin

Born in Los Angeles in 1974, Tom Fruin creates large-scale sculpture and installations using reclaimed materials — quilted steel, salvaged plexiglass, and found objects.

After graduating from UC Santa Barbara in 1996, he relocated to New York City, establishing his studio in the Gowanus neighborhood of Brooklyn, where he continues to live and work.

His monumental works take the form of urban architectural icons — water towers, houses, windmills, flags, and streetcars — elevated into luminous, kaleidoscopic monuments. By day, sunlight passing through colored panels casts prismatic shadows. At night, Arduino-programmed LED lighting transforms each piece into a glowing beacon.

His works are held in collections including The Brooklyn Museum, Museum Kunstpalast (Düsseldorf), the MTA Arts & Design Permanent Collection, and the U.S. Department of State Art in Embassies program.
