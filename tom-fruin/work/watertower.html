Watertower

Year 2012
Medium Found plexiglas, steel, bolts
Dimensions 20 × 10 × 10 feet
Location 20 Jay Street, DUMBO, Brooklyn, NY

A kaleidoscopic rooftop water tower sculpture visible from the parks and streets of DUMBO, the Brooklyn and Manhattan Bridges, FDR Drive, and Lower Manhattan.

Part of Fruin’s Icon series, the Watertower comprises approximately 1,000 scraps of plexiglas sourced from locations across New York City — including Chinatown sign shops, Dennis Oppenheim’s closed studio, and the Build It Green!NYC salvage warehouse.

The sculpture features interior and exterior access ladders and an operable roof hatch. By day, sunlight filters through the multicolored panels, creating shifting prismatic effects. At night, Arduino-controlled light sequences designed by Ryan Holsopple transform it into a glowing beacon on the Brooklyn skyline, with daily shows from dusk onward.

Prime viewing locations include Brooklyn Bridge Park at Washington Street and the Manhattan Bridge bike path. The piece opened June 7th, 2012.
