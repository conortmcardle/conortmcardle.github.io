Kolonihavehus

Year 2010 – Present
Medium Found plexiglas, steel
Dimensions 12 × 12 × 14 feet
Current Location Brooklyn Bridge Park, Brooklyn, NY

A garden house in colorful salvaged plexiglas and steel, inspired by Copenhagen’s modest garden sheds historically provided to state workers. The first sculpture in Fruin’s internationally recognized Icon series.

Composed from roughly one thousand scraps of plexiglas, the Kolonihavehus features a pivot-mounted door with hand-made hinges and several operable windows. Materials were sourced from Copenhagen locations including defunct distributorships, closing shops, and institutional basements.

Since its debut in 2010, the sculpture has toured internationally — from the Royal Danish Library Plaza in Copenhagen to the MuseumsQuartier in Vienna, Prague’s National Theater, and multiple locations across Scandinavia — before finding a permanent home at Brooklyn Bridge Park.

Exhibition History

  • Copenhagen — Royal Danish Library Plaza, Oct–Nov 2010
  • Aalborg, Denmark — Nordkraft, Oct 2011
  • Prague — National Theater Plaza, Apr–May 2012
  • Helsingør, Denmark — Havengade Plaza, Jul–Aug 2012
  • Vienna — MuseumsQuartier, Nov 2012–Jan 2013
  • Brooklyn — Brooklyn Bridge Park, 2014–Present
