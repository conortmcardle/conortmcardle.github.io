Flame

Year 2014
Medium Found glass, lead, copper, steel, lights
Dimensions 8.5 × 11 × 6 feet
Location Brooklyn, NY

A full-scale stained glass replica of the Statue of Liberty’s torch flame, constructed from broken glass sourced from abandoned factory buildings in Detroit.

Fruin hand-pieced hundreds of glass fragments within a lead, copper, and steel framework. The sculpture allows people to get up close to an icon that is normally off limits — the torch’s viewing deck has been inaccessible to the public for over a century.

“I’ve taken the cast-off materials of a broken-down economy and carefully hand-pieced them back together, much like scraps to make a quilt.”

The Flame extends Fruin’s practice of constructing monumental sculptures from salvaged materials, following earlier drug-bag quilts, flags, and the plexiglas-and-steel Icon series.
