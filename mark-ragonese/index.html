The Maker

Biography

Master woodworker, sculptor, and multi-media artist creating one-of-a-kind fine furniture, sculpture, and outdoor site-specific installations from his studio in Bellows Falls, Vermont.

Mark studied at the University of New Hampshire with Daniel Valenza, where he served as studio technician and adjunct faculty. In 1987, he apprenticed with acclaimed sculptor Jon Brooks. He has been a juried member of the League of NH Craftsmen since 1983 and has served as state woodworking juror.

His work has been displayed at the Society of Arts and Crafts in Boston, as well as galleries in New York and Philadelphia. Since 1991, he has been a rostered artist with the NH State Council on the Arts and Very Special Arts. In 1993, he received the Yamagata International Visual Arts Fellowship for his work with disabled and disadvantaged populations.

I feel an energy, a spirit in wood — perhaps its life force — and that life energy continues into every piece I create.

His influences span cultures and centuries: Daniel Valenza, Jon Brooks, Wharton Esherick, Isamu Noguchi, Constantine Brancusi, Modigliani, and the historical makers of many traditions.

Handmade

Each object is designed for the individual client — unique and meaningful. Over 30 years devoted to achieving proficiency in furniture craftsmanship and designing singular pieces.

Process

The process is as important as the product — probably more. Experimentation, creative play, and methodical old-world ways combine in every creation.

Teaching

The Land

Outdoor Works

Bowers, nests, saplings, driftwood, prayer flags, and stone stacks

Bowers, Nests & Saplings

The forest woods have always been a place of shelter and refuge — as well as a playground. Saplings and driftwood become site-specific installations that draw attention to the larger landscape: the topography, the colors, the shadows, and the quality of light.

Prayer Flags

Based loosely on a Himalayan tradition, these installations are delightful, magical, and transformational — turning simple materials into conveyors of hopes and dreams. A space filled with color, life, hope, and possibility.

Stone Stacks

Stone stacking is one of the first forms of creative expression — placing one stone atop another and creating something that would not exist but for you doing it. Through practice, one learns the secrets inside these stones: stillness, balance, slowing down, and attention to detail.

The Teacher

Artist Residencies

Art as a verb rather than a noun

I am a traveling artist. I don't come to teach art as much as do it.

Residencies range from one day to extended periods, emphasizing art as a verb, a process, and creativity as a skill that requires practice. Using natural materials — wood, stone, cloth, and paint — students create meaningful objects while developing individuality and community connection.

Offerings include mural design, stage sets, sculptural gardens, nature trails, sapling structures, prayer flags, storytelling, and performance installations. All residencies are customizable for schools and communities, aligning with curriculum goals.
